\chapter{\abstractname}

This thesis addresses the implementation of dynamic resource management in OpenMP, focusing on the allocation and management of CPU cores---and potentially GPUs---during program execution. Although the \texttt{OMP\_DYNAMIC} flag has been part of the OpenMP standard for over two decades, it has not been fully realized in practice. The work extends parallel regions in LLVM/OpenMP to enable the addition and removal of threads at runtime, thereby improving flexibility and resource utilization. An in-depth analysis of existing thread pool and worker thread mechanisms in libomp is conducted, and the feasibility of integrating a resource manager communication structure is explored. Compatibility with the OpenMP standard is evaluated, with necessary adaptations or deprecations proposed. The integration is designed to be accessible via environment variables or API\@. The effectiveness of the approach is assessed through comprehensive benchmarks, focusing on performance, overhead, and compatibility.
